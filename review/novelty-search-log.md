## 0. Methodology

The review decomposes the paper into atomic claims, each searched independently under at least three distinct vocabularies (the same result may exist under different terminology). For each pivot paper found, citations are chased both backward (its references) and forward (papers citing it). Author sweeps cover researchers most likely to have found each result. Every search is logged with date, query, and outcome, including negatives. The stopping criterion is convergence: when concept searches, citation chases, and author sweeps all return to the same set of neighboring papers with no direct antecedent, diligence is reasonable. Absence is never proven, only made improbable. The final layer is expert contact (the authors of arXiv:2605.08235 and, eventually, referees), whose recognition memory outperforms any search.

## 3. Claim C1: nearest neighbors and distances

N1. Åman-Bengtsson-Pidokrajt 2015 (arXiv:1507.06097). Closest structural precursor. What it does: treats the inner horizon as part of the equilibrium state, studies the two entropy functions S±(M,J) for Kerr, analytically extends the Gibbs surface across the extremal edge using the flat Weinhold metric, shows the combined surface is isometric to the future null cone of two-dimensional Minkowski space, gives unified closed forms for M, S, T± and the Ruppeiner scalar R on the combined surface (their eq. 37), and attempts the analogous extension for Kerr-Newman, leaving it open. What it does not do: it forms no combined invariant of the two branches (no R₊S₊ + R₋S₋ or any analog), makes no constancy or integer statement, contains no mention of entropy products, Ansorg-Hennig, mass independence, or CFT level structure, and involves no complex or virtual roots. Distance: they build the arena (the two-sheet state space) and study its curvature as a field; C1 is the discovery that a specific diagonal contraction on that arena is a constant tied to product universality. Action: cite as the direct precursor of the two-sheet framing; check their eq. (37) against our ν-map for consistency; chase its forward citations (pending).

N2. The entropy-relations school (Xu-Wang-Meng and related, 2013 to 2016). Owns three faces of the four-face equivalence (product, TS relation, central charges) and the Vieta-level treatment of virtual horizons. Distance: no geometry; the geometric face and its equivalence to the other three is absent.

N3. Pradhan corpus (2013 to 2016). Exhaustive algebraic products on both horizons, including Kerr-Sen. Distance: no state-space geometry.

N4. Flatness classification: Åman-Bengtsson-Pidokrajt 2006 (Gen. Rel. Grav. 38, 1305, "Flat information geometries in black hole thermodynamics"): entropy of the form S = M^c1 f(M^c2 X) has flat Ruppeiner metric when c2 = −1, explaining the RN flatness used in Sections 7 and 10. Cite for the structural origin of the RN zero.

N5. Recent activity check: arXiv:2606.18432 (June 2026, warped GMG black holes) uses the Ruppeiner metric on (M,J) with geodesics and curvature invariants; snippet review shows stability analysis, no two-branch invariant, no product connection. Monitor.

N6. Cvetič-Gibbons-Lü-Pope 2018 (arXiv:1806.11134, read in full). The second major structural neighbor. What it does: builds Gibbs surfaces with both branches S± as the two roots of a quadratic in S across many families (Kerr-Newman, STU in four and five dimensions, EMD, dyonic Kaluza-Klein, AdS and dS cases), establishes negative inner-horizon Gibbsian temperatures, derives T₊S₊ + T₋S₋ = 0 in full generality from the inversion symmetry S → K(Q,J)/S of the Christodoulou-Ruffini relation whenever an entropy-product formula S₊S₋ = K(Q,J) holds (their section 4), develops the left and right sector thermodynamics with c_L = c_R, reviews Weinhold and Ruppeiner metrics including the closed Riemann and Ricci formulas for Hessian metrics (their eq. 2.6), exhibits the RN inner-branch Ruppeiner geometry as an infinitely branched covering of the flat plane, and extends the TS relation to the three horizons of the Wu black hole (their eq. 5.34). What it does not do: no curvature invariant is ever combined across branches, R·S appears nowhere, and no connection between curvature and product universality is drawn. On the contrary, the paper explicitly states that the value of the Ricci scalar does not appear in general to have any physical significance. Distance: they own the mirror at the level of the fundamental relation (the inversion symmetry) and the thermodynamic face of the equivalence; the geometric face and its equivalence to the others is exactly what they dismissed as physically insignificant and never computed. C1 is the direct counterpoint to their stated position: a specific diagonal contraction of the Ricci scalar is the detector of product universality.

Consequence 2, a new junction identity discovered by combining Zhang-Gao Theorem 1 with addendum 10.11.
